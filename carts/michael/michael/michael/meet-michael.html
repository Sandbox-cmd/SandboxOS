The checker

His built-in safety inspector

3D printers are picky. Before you commit, Michael runs a quick inspection and answers the questions that decide whether a print succeeds:

🔍

Is it the size you think it is?

A stray setting can make a part come out a hundred times too big — he catches that first.

🧱

Is it one solid piece?

A print has to be sealed and whole — no gaps or loose bits — or it falls apart.

📏

Are the walls thick enough?

Too thin and it snaps. He measures the whole part, not just a lucky spot.

📦

Will it fit the printer?

He checks it fits your printer’s bed — a Bambu Lab A1, about 25 cm each way — before you start.

📐

Any tricky overhangs?

Steep undersides may need support or a flip — he flags them early.

🧊

Are the curves smooth enough?

If the model is built coarser than the printer can lay down, curves print faceted — he says so.

PLA+
stiff & easy — everyday parts
TPU
bendy & rubbery — grips & guards
PETG
tough & sturdy — hard-working parts

Tell him what you’re printing with and he checks against that material’s rules — a bendy part needs thicker walls than a stiff one. And he measures instead of guessing by eye.

These three are the everyday ones; he knows eight in all. A couple of things he’ll tell you himself: PLA+ follows plain PLA’s rules — the difference is printer settings, not shape — and the tougher engineering plastics (ABS, ASA, PC, nylon) want an enclosed printer, which the open-frame A1 isn’t.
