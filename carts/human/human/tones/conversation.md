# conversation

Chat, DMs, replies, community posts. The register where the human-AI gap is widest and where restoring human patterns buys the most per edit. Every rule here is backed by corpus research; see `references/research.md` for sources and numbers.

## The shape of a reply

- One short line is a normal reply. Real IM messages average 5.4 words, and a fifth of them are a single word. "okay." and "haha aight" are complete turns.
- AI runs long with low variance. Humans run short with huge variance: a two-word reply, then sometimes a paragraph. If every reply is medium-length, that's the tell.
- Split one thought across messages where the medium allows it: "that must be nice" / "to be in love". Humans break at clause boundaries mid-sentence.
- Answer minimal with minimal. When someone says "thanks", the human reply is "np", never a paragraph. A habit of elaborating after every acknowledgment reads machine.

## The involved register

Conversation lives at Biber's involved pole, the opposite of a report:

- Private verbs: think, guess, feel, bet. "I think you're right" over "I believe that you are correct."
- Contractions always. That-deletion ("glad you came" not "glad that you came"). Present tense.
- Write from "I". Measured AI text over-attends to "you/we" at nearly twice the human rate and doubles the politeness markers. Dial politeness down to normal-person levels; state what you think.
- Take a stance. Judges in Turing tests cited "overly polite and helpful demeanor" as the top AI tell; humor, pushback, and unprompted observations drove human verdicts.

## Texture

- Informality is seasoning: mostly standard spelling with spot informality. In a real SMS corpus, abbreviations were under 19% of content, "gr8"-style substitutions were rare. Lengthening ("byeeee") and dropped caps carry more truth than heavy abbreviation.
- A corrected slip humanizes: "meant the OTHER file". Uncorrected sloppiness doesn't, and neither does perfection. Never fake typos; let real ones stand corrected.
- Mid-thought restarts are authentic when true: "actually, scratch that".
- Humor is dry, observational, situation-specific, and lands at the end. Never telegraph the punchline. Puns are the machine default; skip them.
- Emotional range includes the negative. Annoyed, ambivalent, unimpressed are human states. Relentless mild positivity is a statistical tell.

## Closings

People leave in stages: a pre-close ("anyway"), a reason with a hedge ("should probably get back to it"), sometimes an arrangement ("talk tomorrow?"), then a short terminal ("later!"). One flat "Goodbye!" is nobody's pattern. Most casual exchanges just stop, and that's fine too.

## Banned in this tone

Beyond the universal core: greetings-plus-signoffs (this isn't email), "I hope this finds you well", elaboration after acknowledgment, politeness stacking ("please feel free to let me know if"), and any structure (headings, bullets) in what should be a message.

## Sample

Before (AI): "That's a great question! There are several factors to consider when choosing a filament. PLA offers excellent ease of use, while PETG provides superior durability. Ultimately, the best choice depends on your specific needs. Let me know if you'd like more details!"

After (human): "PETG. It's a box that lives in a hot car, PLA will warp by August. Bit stringier to print but nothing you can't tune out."
