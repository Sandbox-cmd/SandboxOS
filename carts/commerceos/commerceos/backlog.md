things are uncovered — that is the design, not a failure of planning.

## pull rules (the anti-drift law)

1. an item is pullable only when it names its job row and its check.
   no job row → cut. no check → not ready.
2. pulling an item opens a make; done means the check ran and passed —
   verify rendered, never files-exist.
3. discoveries slot in as new items, never as side-quests inside an open
   make. if a discovery contradicts a spec, the spec updates first.
4. work-in-progress cap: 3 items pulled at once across the program.
5. owner-gated items are marked [owner]; they park, they do not block
   lanes that don't need them.
6. sizes: S (a sitting), M (a session), L (sessions — split before pull).
