## what this is

The operator interface evolved into a collaboration surface: the owner
and claude code side by side, running the stores commerceos operates.
His frame, verbatim: "the commerce OS interface that I the store
operator will interact with, monitor, view and check up on my stores on
alongside having Claude Code with Commerce OS running on the side so it
becomes a collaboration surface to run the store."

Serves the O rows (spec/jtbd.md) plus O-collab (named in the SF1 find):
when claude code is running the store, I want to see what it is doing
now, what it is about to do, and what it needs from me — in the same
place I monitor the stores.

## the direction (the fusion: quiet page × desk)

- **The quiet page carries the frame.** The resting state is a
  near-empty page that speaks in sentences. Silence is the product: a
  well-run day renders as "Nothing needs you." plus one calm line.
- **The sentence is the triage.** The first line names the size of the
  day and where to spend the eyes: "Two things need you." / "Eleven
  things need you. Three deserve your eyes. Eight are routine and can
  go together." No widget wall does this work.
- **The desk contributes the unit.** Every piece of work is a ticket:
  title, one meta line, a colored edge that means one thing —
  amber: waits on you · blue: in the machine's hands · red: stopped
  honestly · green: landed. Depth opens ON the ticket (receipt in
  place); it never sits on the page.
- **Under load, group by reversibility.** Money and irreversible moves
  stay individual tickets under "your eyes first"; reversible routine
  collapses into ONE honest batch ticket riding the batch-approve loop
  (was→becomes preview, one glance-approve). A stopped job gets its own
  group with its receipt: what stopped, why, what was not touched.
- **Two screens.** The wall: sit down, the sentence, waiting tickets,
  one calm line per store, doors. The board (open a store): two zones —
  my side (running tickets with progress) and your side (waiting
  tickets) — landed below, doors to catalog/money/checks/agents/record.

## the register (this surface's own)

Supersedes the 2026-07-12 teletext ruling FOR THIS SURFACE (the
chronology law: the record keeps both; other surfaces keep teletext
until ruled otherwise). Light ground, system type stack, sentence-first
hierarchy, tickets with soft depth and a 3px colored edge. No broadcast
fiction, no page numbers, no terminal cosplay. Plain words only — the
plain-language guard walks this surface like every other.

## the laws carried (earned, not costume — they survive any restyle)

1. Nothing lands without the owner; approving is one gesture per ticket
   (or one honest batch), through the same gate as everything else.
2. Every claim opens to its receipt, rendered in place.
3. Every mirror-number wears its age ("health 78.5 as of last night").
4. Counts are computed at render — real counts, not claims.
5. One focus per screen; one reading direction; depth on the unit.
6. Refusals and stops are first-class tickets with reasons, never hidden.
7. The machine speaks of its own acts in first person, plainly.
